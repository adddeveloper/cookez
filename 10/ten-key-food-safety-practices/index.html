Ten Key Food Safety Practices

  1. Clean Hands and Surfaces

    Always wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water before handling food, and after using the bathroom, handling pets, or changing diapers. Use separate cutting boards for raw meat, poultry, and seafood, and wash all cutting boards, utensils, and surfaces with hot, soapy water after each use.

    Source: CDC - Keep Food Safe

  2. Cook Foods to Proper Temperatures

    Use a food thermometer to ensure that meat, poultry, seafood, and eggs are cooked to a safe internal temperature. Cook ground beef to at least 160°F, poultry to at least 165°F, and seafood to at least 145°F. Reheat leftovers to at least 165°F.

    Source: FDA - Using Food Thermometers

  3. Avoid Cross-Contamination

    Keep raw meat, poultry, and seafood separate from other foods in your grocery cart, refrigerator, and during food preparation. Use separate cutting boards, utensils, and dishes for raw and cooked foods.

    Source: FoodSafety.gov - Food Safety Myths vs. Facts

  4. Refrigerate Promptly

    Refrigerate perishable foods, such as meat, poultry, seafood, and dairy products, within two hours of cooking or purchasing. Keep your refrigerator at 40°F or below and your freezer at 0°F or below.

    Source: FoodSafety.gov - 4 Steps to Food Safety

  5. Use Safe Water and Raw Materials

    Use safe, clean water for drinking, cooking, and washing. Choose fresh, unspoiled ingredients and avoid using ingredients that are past their expiration date or that have an unusual odor or appearance.

    Source: WHO - Food Safety

  6. Store Foods Properly

    Store foods at the proper temperature and in the proper location. Keep raw meat, poultry, and seafood in a separate, sealed container on the bottom shelf of your refrigerator. Keep canned goods in a cool, dry place and do not use cans that are dented, swollen, or corroded.

    Source: FoodSafety.gov - 4 Steps to Food Safety

  7. Be Aware of Foodborne Illness Symptoms

    Be aware of the symptoms of foodborne illness, such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and fever. Seek medical attention if you experience these symptoms after consuming food, especially if you are in a high-risk group such as pregnant women, young children, older adults, or individuals with weakened immune systems.

    Source: CDC - Foodborne Illness Symptoms

  8. Use Safe Cooking Methods

    Use safe cooking methods, such as boiling, steaming, baking, and roasting, to cook food thoroughly and kill harmful bacteria. Avoid consuming raw or undercooked meat, poultry, seafood, and eggs.

    Source: FoodSafety.gov - Safe Minimum Cooking Temperature

  9. Practice Safe Food Handling Outside the Home

    When eating out, choose restaurants that practice safe food handling and preparation methods. If you have leftovers, refrigerate them promptly and reheat them to a safe temperature before consuming.

    Source: FDA - Tips for Eating Out

  10. Be Mindful of High-Risk Foods

    Be mindful of high-risk foods that are more likely to cause foodborne illness, such as raw or undercooked eggs, raw or undercooked meat and poultry, raw or undercooked seafood, unpasteurized dairy products, and fresh produce that has not been washed properly.

    Source: FoodSafety.gov - 4 Steps to Food Safety
