Ten Food Trends

  1. Plant-Based Foods

    Plant-based foods are becoming increasingly popular, as more people are looking for healthier and more sustainable food options. This includes plant-based proteins, such as tofu, tempeh, and seitan, as well as plant-based milk alternatives, like almond, soy, and oat milk. (Source: Forbes)

  2. Alternative Flours

    Alternative flours, such as almond, coconut, and cassava flour, are becoming more popular among people who are gluten-free or looking for lower-carb options. These flours can be used to make a variety of baked goods, from bread to cookies. (Source: Healthline)

  3. Flexitarianism

    Flexitarianism is a diet that focuses on plant-based foods, but allows for occasional consumption of meat and other animal products. This approach is gaining popularity as more people are looking for a more balanced and sustainable way of eating. (Source: Medical News Today)

  4. Functional Foods

    Functional foods are foods that have health benefits beyond their basic nutritional value. This includes foods like probiotics, which support gut health, and foods fortified with vitamins and minerals, like breakfast cereals. (Source: Harvard Health Blog)

  5. Low-Alcohol Drinks

    Low-alcohol drinks, such as hard seltzers and low-proof cocktails, are becoming more popular among people who want to enjoy a drink without the full effects of alcohol. These drinks are often lower in calories and sugar as well. (Source: Eater)

  6. Regenerative Agriculture

    Regenerative agriculture is a farming method that focuses on building soil health and biodiversity, while also producing food. This approach is gaining popularity as more people are looking for sustainable and environmentally-friendly food options. (Source: The Nature Conservancy)

  7. Local and Seasonal Foods

    Local and seasonal foods are becoming more popular as people are looking to support local farmers and reduce their carbon footprint. This includes fruits, vegetables, and meats that are grown or raised nearby and are in season. (Source: The New York Times)

  8. Food Waste Reduction

    Reducing food waste is becoming more important as people become more aware of the environmental and economic costs of throwing away food. This includes efforts to compost, donate, or repurpose food that might otherwise go to waste. (Source: Food Network)

  9. Innovative Snacks

    Snacks are becoming more creative, with new flavors and ingredients that appeal to a wider range of tastes. This includes snacks made with alternative flours, like chickpea flour crackers, as well as savory snacks like seaweed chips. (Source: Delish)

  10. Virtual Restaurants

    Virtual restaurants are delivery-only restaurants that operate out of a commercial kitchen, often without a storefront. These restaurants allow chefs to experiment with new concepts and offer unique dining experiences. (Source: Food & Wine)

  11. CBD-Infused Foods

    CBD-infused foods, such as gummies, drinks, and snacks, are becoming more popular as more people look to CBD for its potential health benefits, like reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation. (Source: Eat This, Not That!)
