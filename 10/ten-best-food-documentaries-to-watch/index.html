Top Ten Best Food Documentaries to Watch

Discover the fascinating and complex world of food with these thought-provoking and inspiring documentaries. From food politics to culinary history, these films are a must-watch for any food lover.

  1. Food, Inc. (2008)

    A powerful expose on the American food industry, exploring the impacts of industrialized farming and the consequences for our health and environment.

  2. Jiro Dreams of Sushi (2011)

    A stunning portrait of Jiro Ono, an 85-year-old sushi master in Tokyo, and his dedication to the art of sushi-making.

  3. Barbecue (2017)

    A mouth-watering exploration of the culture and community surrounding barbecue, from the American South to Australia and beyond.

  4. City of Gold (2015)

    A love letter to the vibrant and diverse food scene of Los Angeles, as seen through the eyes of food critic Jonathan Gold.

  5. Chef's Table (2015-)

    A series of intimate and inspiring portraits of the world's most renowned chefs, exploring their culinary philosophies and personal journeys.

  6. Salt Fat Acid Heat (2018)

    A beautiful and informative series exploring the fundamentals of cooking through the lens of author and chef Samin Nosrat.

  7. The Birth of Sake (2015)

    A rare and fascinating glimpse into the traditional art of sake-making in a small Japanese brewery.

  8. Cowspiracy (2014)

    A groundbreaking documentary examining the impact of animal agriculture on the environment, and the industries' attempts to conceal it.

  9. Ugly Delicious (2018-)

    A humorous and informative series exploring the cultural and social aspects of food, hosted by renowned chef David Chang.

  10. Somm (2012)

    An intense and riveting look at the elusive world of Master Sommeliers, and their quest to achieve the highest level of wine knowledge and tasting ability.
